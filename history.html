Top historical places in India

Qutb Minar (Delhi)

The Qutub Minar is a 73-meter tall minaret built in the 12th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is known for its Islamic architecture and beautiful red sandstone structure, along with the nearby Iron Pillar that dates back over 1,500 years.

Red Fort (Delhi)

Constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan, the Red Fort is an imposing structure made of red sandstone. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is a symbol of India's independence, where the Prime Minister hoists the national flag annually.

Mehrangarh Fort (Jodhpur, Rajasthan)

One of India’s largest forts, Mehrangarh is perched atop a hill, offering panoramic views of Jodhpur’s “Blue City.” Built in the 15th century by Rao Jodha, the fort is known for its massive walls, intricate palaces, and museum showcasing royal artifacts.
