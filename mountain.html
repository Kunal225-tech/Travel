Top mountains in India

Manali (Himachal Pradesh)

Nestled in the Kullu Valley, Manali is famous for its snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and adventure sports like paragliding, river rafting, and trekking. The nearby Solang Valley and Rohtang Pass are popular spots for winter sports.

Shimla (Himachal Pradesh)

Once the summer capital of British India, Shimla is known for its colonial architecture, vibrant markets, and scenic landscapes. The Ridge, Mall Road, and Jakhoo Temple are popular attractions, and it serves as a great base for exploring nearby hill stations.

Nainital (Uttarakhand)

Known as the “Lake District of India,” Nainital is surrounded by mountain peaks and famous for Naini Lake, Naina Devi Temple, and Tiffin Top. This picturesque hill station is ideal for boating, trekking, and exploring the scenic beauty of the Kumaon region.
